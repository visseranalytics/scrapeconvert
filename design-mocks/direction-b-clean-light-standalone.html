Scraper

Pull every image off a page without saving them one by one.

Paste a single URL, a list of URLs, or a sitemap. ScrapeConvert reads each page and collects the images it finds. From there you can sort the set by size and trim it down before you do anything with it.

  • URLs or a whole sitemap

    Feed it one page or point it at a sitemap to walk a site. It queues the pages and reports what it finds.

  • Filter and catch duplicates

    Narrow the list by format or size. When the same image shows up twice, it gets flagged so it doesn't clutter your batch.

  • Hand the selection to the converter

    Tick the images you want and send them straight into the converter. No re-downloading, no file juggling.

Browsers block scripts from reading another site's images directly, so the fetches run through our own proxy to get around that limit. The proxy is rate-limited, asks for a quick one-time bot check per session, and pulls public images only. It skips paid stock-photo sites and private addresses.

Converter

Convert a whole batch, then download one ZIP.

Drop in your own files or use the images you scraped. Choose a format, set the quality, resize if you need to, and convert the whole set at once. The work happens in your browser using WebAssembly codecs, so nothing gets uploaded.

Privacy

Here is exactly what touches a server, and what does not.

We would rather be precise than make a tidy promise we can't keep. The two halves of the tool work differently, so here is the honest version.

Converting stays local

When you convert an image, it is read and rewritten inside your browser. The image bytes do not leave your device, and there is no upload step. Close the tab and nothing is left behind on our end, because nothing was ever sent.

Scraping passes through a proxy

Scraping is different, and we won't pretend otherwise. The URL you enter and the page and image bytes we fetch travel through our Cloudflare Worker proxy. That is the only way around the browser's cross-origin block. We don't store any of it; the proxy fetches, hands the result back, and forgets it.

Analytics are privacy-friendly and aggregate only. No ad networks, no third-party trackers, no selling data. You can read the proxy code and the whole app on GitHub.

FAQ

Questions, answered plainly.

Is it really free?

Yes. ScrapeConvert is free and open source under the MIT license. There is no paid tier and no upsell. If it saves you time, there is a buy-me-a-coffee link, but it is optional.

Do you store my images?

Conversion happens in your browser, so those files are never uploaded or saved. Scraping is the exception: the pages and images we fetch for you pass through our proxy to get around browser limits. We don't keep them. The proxy fetches, returns the result, and holds nothing.

Why does scraping need a proxy?

Browsers won't let a script on our page read the contents of another site directly. That rule is called CORS, and it exists for good security reasons. To fetch a page's images, the request has to come from a server instead, so we run a small proxy that does exactly that and nothing more.

Is the scraping respectful?

We try to be a good citizen. The proxy only pulls public images, skips paid stock-photo sites, and refuses private or internal addresses. It is rate-limited, and each session does a one-time bot check so the service isn't abused at scale. Use it for images you have the right to use.

Can I self-host it?

Yes. The app and the proxy worker are built to run on Cloudflare, and the repo includes what you need to deploy your own copy. Run it with your own rate limits and bot check, and you control the whole pipeline.

Is there a browser extension?

There is a free Chrome extension that scrapes the images on whatever tab you are looking at, then drops them into the same converter. It is handy when you are already on the page you want.
